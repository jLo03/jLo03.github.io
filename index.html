Why Bugs?

We should all eat bugs. Eating insects can have a positive effect on your life in a variety of ways. There is the personal experience, the global experience and the environmental impact that eating insects can have.

  • Adding insects to our diets will bring out new tastes and textures with a whole new palate experience.
  • Insects are nutritious and packed with protein that has all of the essential amino acids. They are high in iron, calcium and B12. They are a great source of Omega 3 and are prebiotics (nutrition for probiotics).
  • edible insects bullet Meat production is a major cause of global warming and is unsustainable. Adding insects to diets worldwide will reduce the need for meat and therefore help fight global warming.

    • Eating Bugs is Good for Us and Good for the Planet!

Grasshopper

Nicknamed "chapulines", they are a popular delicacy in Mexico. Tastes like shrimp! Contains 30g of protein and 3.8g of fat per Grasshopper

Cricket

Served on a skewer, it was reported that Goshute Indians named shrimps "sea cricket" when encountering them! Has 9.6g of protein and 5.6g of fat per Cricket.

Wasp Larve

A popular delicacy in Japan, so much that it needs to be imported from other countries to meet demand. Contains 17g of protein and 3.6g of fat.

Witchetty grub

Otherwise known as "Hoo Hoo grub", it is traditionally eaten by Aboriginals. Tastes like chicken when cooked! Contains 16g of protein and 29g of fat per grub.
